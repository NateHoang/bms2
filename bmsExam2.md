| Specific Virus        | Family / Group | Genome Type & Polarity        | Clinical Effects & Key Notes                                                                                      |
| :-------------------- | :------------- | :---------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Adenovirus**        | Adeno          | **dsDNA** (Non-enveloped)     | Respiratory (colds, pneumonia), ocular (conjunctivitis), and GI (diarrhea) infections.                            |
| **Hepatitis A (HAV)** | Picorna        | **(+) ssRNA** (Non-enveloped) | Acute, self-limiting hepatitis; transmitted via **fecal-oral** route; jaundice is common.                         |
| **Hepatitis B (HBV)** | Hepadna        | **dsDNA** (Enveloped)         | Acute and **chronic liver disease** (cirrhosis, liver cancer); bloodborne/sexual transmission; vaccine available. |
| **Hepatitis C (HCV)** | Flavi          | **(+) ssRNA** (Enveloped)     | High rate of **chronic infection** (~80%); can lead to cirrhosis and liver cancer; high genetic variation.        |
| **Hepatitis D (HDV)** | Satellite      | **(-) ssRNA** (Enveloped)     | Severe liver disease; **defective virus**—only causes infection if Hepatitis B (HBsAg) is present.                |
| **Hepatitis E (HEV)** | Hepe           | **(+) ssRNA** (Non-enveloped) | Fecal-oral; similar to HAV but has a **high mortality rate (20%) in pregnant females**.                           |
| **RSV**               | Paramyxo       | **(-) ssRNA** (Enveloped)     | Severe respiratory symptoms (bronchiolitis) in **infants**, elderly, and immunocompromised.                       |
| **HIV (1 & 2)**       | Retro          | **(+) ssRNA** (Enveloped)     | Depletion of **CD4+ T cells**; leads to AIDS; uses **Reverse Transcriptase** to integrate into host genome.       |
| **Oncogenic Viruses** | Various        | **DNA or RNA**                | Causes **malignant transformation** (cancer); examples: HPV (Cervical), EBV (Burkitt), HBV/HCV (Liver).           |
